# ODS — Operational Decision Standard

An open specification for interoperable, auditable, and governable decision systems.

---

## Overview

The Operational Decision Standard (ODS) defines a standardized schema and governance framework for institutional decision memory.

ODS enables organizations to:
- record decisions with temporal integrity
- preserve institutional context
- support auditability and compliance
- improve decision quality over time
- establish interoperable governance workflows

ODS is designed for environments where decision quality, accountability, and traceability are operationally critical.

---

## Why ODS Exists

Modern organizations maintain sophisticated infrastructure for:
- data storage
- analytics
- observability
- security
- compliance

However, most organizations lack standardized infrastructure for decision governance.

As a result:
- critical decisions are poorly documented
- institutional memory is fragmented
- auditability is inconsistent
- accountability becomes difficult
- learning loops degrade over time

ODS addresses this gap by defining a common specification layer for decision systems.

---

## Core Principles

ODS implementations are designed around six foundational principles:

1. Immutability  
2. Verifiability  
3. Attribution  
4. Temporal Integrity  
5. Explainability  
6. Outcome Tracking

These principles establish the foundation for trustworthy decision infrastructure.

---

## Specification Scope

ODS defines:
- decision schemas
- governance metadata
- audit structures
- temporal integrity requirements
- interoperability patterns
- compliance models

ODS does NOT define:
- proprietary runtimes
- business logic
- model architectures
- organizational policy
